
# Listening and Active Communication — Rephrased Answers

## Question 1  
**What are the strategies for Active Listening?**  
- Avoid letting your own thoughts distract you.  
- Stay focused on the speaker and the topic.  
- Don’t interrupt while the other person is speaking.  
- Allow them to finish completely before responding.  
- Use positive and attentive body language.  
- Take notes when it feels appropriate.

---

## Question 2  
**Key points of Reflective Listening (Fisher’s model):**  
- Match or mirror the other person's body language.  
- Repeat or summarize what you understood to confirm accuracy.  
- Respond with empathy instead of jumping to disagreement.  
- Encourage them to express more by staying supportive.

---

## Question 3  
**Obstacles in my listening process:**  
- Sometimes the topic doesn't interest me.  
- I try too hard to make a good impression and start planning my reply while they're still speaking.

---

## Question 4  
**How I can improve my listening:**  
- Stay genuinely engaged in conversations.  
- Maintain eye contact.  
- Ask questions whenever something is unclear.  
- Practice being a better listener in everyday interactions.

---

## Question 5  
**When I switch to a Passive communication style:**  
- When I’m unsure or lack confidence about the topic.  
- When someone is angry, and I stay silent to avoid conflict.  
- When I feel speaking up won’t make a difference.  

---

## Question 6  
**When I shift into an Aggressive communication style:**  
- When I feel I must strongly defend myself.  
- When the other person doesn’t even try to understand my viewpoint.  
- When emotions run high and I lose calm.

---

## Question 7  
**When I become Passive-Aggressive (sarcasm, gossip, taunts, silent treatment, etc.):**  
- When I’m hurt but avoid confronting directly.  
- When bottled-up frustration from being passive comes out indirectly.  
- When I feel unsafe or unable to express myself openly.

---

## Question 8  
**How I can communicate more assertively:**  
- Maintain steady eye contact and an open, relaxed posture.  
- Speak at a natural volume—neither too loud nor too soft.  
- Stay calm, composed, and confident.  
- Ask clearly for what I want instead of hinting subtly.  
- Listen attentively and avoid interrupting.
