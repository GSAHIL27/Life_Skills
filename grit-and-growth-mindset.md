## Question 3 – What is the Internal Locus of Control? What is the key point in the video? 
An internal locus of control is the belief that you — through your own actions, choices and effort — have control over what happens in your life. People with this mindset assume that outcomes (successes or failures) are largely the result of their own behavior. 

The main idea of the video is that adopting an internal locus of control — believing that you can influence your life through your effort and decisions — helps you stay motivated. The “Locus Rule” encourages you to focus on what you can control (your actions, attitude, choices) rather than external factors (luck, circumstances), which empowers you to take responsibility and keep moving forward even when things get hard.

## Question 4 – Key Points to Build a Growth Mindset (No Explanation)

- Believe in your ability to figure things out. 
- Accept that you can improve with effort and learning.
- Embrace challenges rather than avoiding them. 
- View failures/mistakes as opportunities to learn and grow. 
- Prioritize effort, persistence and consistent practice. 
- Seek feedback and learn from criticism rather than rejecting it. 
- Keep learning new things — be curious and open to growth.

## Question 5 – Your Ideas to Build a Growth Mindset (Software Developer Perspective)

- Practice coding consistently, even when it feels difficult. 
- Take mistakes in code as learning signals rather than failures. 
- Ask for feedback during code reviews and apply it sincerely. 
- Break complex problems into smaller steps and solve them patiently. 
- Learn one new concept, tool, or technology every week. 
- Challenge yourself with slightly harder problems or projects regularly. 
- Reflect on what you learned at the end of each day or week. 
- Stay curious, experiment more, and don’t be afraid to try new approaches.
